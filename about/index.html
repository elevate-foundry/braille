About the BrailleBuddy Ecosystem

Our Mission

"To revolutionize braille accessibility through innovative technology, creating a comprehensive ecosystem that empowers visually impaired individuals while establishing new standards for digital trust and identity verification."

The BrailleBuddy Ecosystem represents a groundbreaking approach to braille accessibility and digital identity. What began as an educational game has evolved into a comprehensive platform that addresses multiple challenges in accessibility, data encoding, trust verification, and identity management.

Our work spans four interconnected components, each building upon the others to create a seamless and secure experience for users of all abilities.

BrailleBuddy Game

The original BrailleBuddy is an educational game designed to teach braille in an engaging and interactive way. It provides a foundation for understanding braille patterns and their meanings, making learning accessible to people of all ages and abilities.

BBES (BrailleBuddy Encoding System)

Building on the patterns used in braille, BBES is a revolutionary encoding system that leverages these patterns for efficient data compression and representation. This system enables secure identity verification and compact data storage while preserving semantic meaning.

MOTL (Metrics of Trust and Legitimacy)

MOTL provides advanced trust scoring tools and verification mechanisms to establish legitimacy in digital interactions. By analyzing multiple factors including device consistency, behavioral patterns, and interaction history, MOTL creates comprehensive trust profiles for digital identities.

BBID (BrailleBuddy Identity)

BBID is our innovative identity verification system that uses behavioral fingerprinting and device characteristics to create unique digital identities. It eliminates the need for traditional passwords while providing enhanced security through continuous authentication.

Our Approach to Behavioral Fingerprinting

A cornerstone of our ecosystem is the comprehensive behavioral fingerprinting strategy implemented in BBID. This approach captures unique user interaction patterns across different devices:

  • Device-specific metrics: We collect different behavioral data depending on the device type, including keyboard dynamics and mouse behavior on laptops, touchscreen interactions on smartphones, and touch and stylus interactions on tablets.
  • Privacy-preserving: All behavioral data is processed with privacy as a core principle, ensuring sensitive information is never exposed or stored in ways that could compromise security.
  • Continuous verification: Rather than relying on one-time authentication, our system continuously monitors behavioral patterns to maintain ongoing verification throughout user sessions.

This approach allows us to create highly accurate identity profiles while respecting user privacy and providing a seamless experience across devices.

Project Timeline

2023 Q1

BrailleBuddy Game Launch

Initial release of the BrailleBuddy educational game, focused on teaching braille patterns through interactive gameplay.

2023 Q3

BBES Development

Research and development of the BrailleBuddy Encoding System, exploring how braille patterns could be leveraged for efficient data representation.

2024 Q1

BBID Prototype

First prototype of the BBID identity verification system, introducing behavioral fingerprinting concepts.

2024 Q2

MOTL Integration

Development of trust metrics and integration with BBID to create comprehensive trust profiles.

2024 Q4

API Deployment

Deployment of the BBID API to Vercel, enabling integration with third-party applications.

2025 Q1

Ecosystem Restructuring

Comprehensive restructuring of the BrailleBuddy ecosystem to improve navigation, standardize design, and enhance user experience across all components.
