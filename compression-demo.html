Braille: The Ultimate Compression Format for Language

Braille is more than just a tactile writing system—it's one of the most efficient data compression formats for written language ever created.

This demo explores how Braille compresses language and demonstrates how we can enhance this compression even further with haptic feedback and AI optimization.

6 Dots = 26 Letters

Braille uses just 6 dots to encode the entire alphabet, numbers, and punctuation. This is remarkably space-efficient compared to visual text.

Grade 2 Contractions

Grade 2 Braille uses contractions to represent common words and letter combinations. For example, "the" is just one character (⠮) instead of three.

Haptic Compression

Our haptic engine takes compression further by encoding words as unique vibration patterns inspired by biological rhythms.
