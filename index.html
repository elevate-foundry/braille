About Braille

Braille is a tactile writing system used by people who are visually impaired. It was invented by Louis Braille in 1824, who was blind himself.

Each braille character or "cell" is made up of 6 dots arranged in a 2×3 grid. By raising different combinations of these dots, different letters, numbers, and symbols can be represented.

Why Learn Braille?

Learning braille promotes:

  • Empathy and understanding for visually impaired people
  • Cognitive development and pattern recognition
  • Tactile sensitivity and fine motor skills
  • Accessibility awareness from an early age

BrailleBuddy aims to make learning braille fun and accessible for sighted children, fostering inclusivity and awareness.
