About Grade 2 Braille

Grade 2 Braille is a more advanced form of braille that uses contractions to represent common letter combinations and words. This makes braille reading faster and reduces the size of braille books.

For example, the word "the" can be written with a single character instead of three separate letters.

About Braille

Braille is a tactile writing system used by people who are visually impaired. It was invented by Louis Braille in 1824, who was blind himself.

Each braille character or "cell" is made up of 6 dots arranged in a 2×3 grid. By raising different combinations of these dots, different letters, numbers, and symbols can be represented.

Why Learn Braille?

Learning braille promotes:

  • Empathy and understanding for visually impaired people
  • Cognitive development and pattern recognition
  • Tactile sensitivity and fine motor skills
  • Accessibility awareness from an early age

BrailleBuddy aims to make learning braille fun and accessible for sighted children, fostering inclusivity and awareness.

Haptic Feedback

Standard mode provides a simple dot-by-dot vibration pattern that directly corresponds to the braille cell.

Rhythmic mode creates patterns with varying durations that help distinguish between different characters.

Frequency-based mode varies the intensity of vibrations based on the number and position of dots in the braille cell.

Biological Contractions mode mimics physiological contractions with wave-like patterns that build up, peak, and taper off. This creates an intuitive tactile experience for learning braille contractions.

Custom mode allows you to create and save your own vibration patterns for each braille character.
